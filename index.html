The Problem

A foreigner cannot simply register a mining company in Peru and run it alone

Peruvian law requires legal presence, local representation, and procedures across multiple agencies — SUNARP, INGEMMET, SUNAT, Migraciones, environmental and sectoral authorities. Without knowing the system, every step turns into lost months or costly mistakes.

1
Legal Entry Barrier

Without residency, you cannot officially be the director of your own company from day one.

2
Fragmented Agencies

SUNARP, INGEMMET, SUNAT, OSINERGMIN, ANA and Migraciones are not connected. Coordinating them manually takes months.

3
Risks of Untested Concessions & Permits

A concession without due diligence, exports without licenses, or working with mercury without an IQBF permit is a direct path to fines and project shutdown.

Frequently Asked Questions

What Clients Ask Us Most

Can I be the director of my company from day one?

No, until you obtain the carné de extranjería. During the transition period, we act as your temporary legal representative under a contract with clearly defined terms and transfer conditions.

What if the concession I'm interested in is near the border?

Special permits for foreigners are required for such zones (Article 71 of the Peruvian Constitution). This is checked during the diagnosis phase before any work begins.

Is a separate permit required for using mercury?

Yes, if your mining process involves controlled chemical substances — IQBF registration with SUNAT is required.

Do you help with selling mined minerals?

Yes, as a separate service block — export documentation and direct access to verified wholesale buyers.
